An Open Letter...

Update: It looks as though the suspension has been rescinded. Thank you to President Monaco for intervening and helping to resolve the situation.

Edit: I have updated this letter to address it to both Dean Reitman and Director of Rowing, Gary Caldwell. Recent reports indicate that the suspension may have been an internal decision as opposed to a top-down decision. Regardless, I still strongly disagree with the decision to suspend the team for the reasons outlined below and request that whoever has the power to do so reverse the decision immediately.

Dear Director Caldwell and Dean Reitman,

I am a recent Tufts Alum, having graduated Magna Cum Laude with a Bachelors of Science in Electrical Engineering last year. During my time at Tufts, I served as the the General Manager of WMFO from 2009 through 2011 and counted a number of Tufts rowers among my close friends and associates. I am now pursing my doctorate in Computer Science at the University of Colorado.

I recently became aware of the suspension of a number of members of the Tufts Men's crew team in relation to an incident involving their selection of custom jerseys for last weekend's Spring Fling event as reported in the May 2nd issue of the Tufts Daily. I am writing to express my disappointment and disagreement with the decision to suspend these individuals, and to request that you reconsider or overrule this decision.

The jerseys in question displayed the phrase "check out our cox" above a standard image of a coxswain piloted crew boat. Having run a broadcast FM radio station for two years, I am more familiar than most with the standards our society applies when classifying obscene and offensive material, as well as the means by which we arrive at such standards. In general, our society operates under the idea that speech must be considered offensive by a protected member of the expected audience in order to forfeit the right to free expression. This is why the late George Carlin can say "fuck" on television after 10PM at night but not during the day. I do not believe that the jersey design in question would be reasonably found to be offensive by a member of the audience at a typical Spring Fling event. There may be many instances in which such jerseys would not be appropriate, but Spring Fling is not one of them.

Furthermore, matters of offense are highly subjective. We have a duty to insure that all reasonable complaints are investigated and considered. But we also have a duty to uphold the rights of the accused. Due to the highly subjective nature of matters of offense, it is very important that judgment be passed not by an individual, but by a jury of peers. Those wishing to register complaints should have the opportunity to do so, but the proper response to these complaints should be decided by the TCUJ or a similar student body, not by the Office of Student Affairs, the Athletic department, or the team's coach. Membership on a athletic team does not forfeit one's right to free expression, especially while taking part in activities that are not sponsored by the team. The members of the men's crew team should be given adequate opportunity to defend their right to free expression. They should be treated as innocent until their peers find them guilty. They should not be suspended for subjectively offensive actions on the basis of a single set of complaints at the judgment of a single office or individual.

Most importantly, this suspension is a disservice to those of us that fight daily against a wide range of truly offensive, misogynistic material that supports a cultural acceptance of sexual violence and rape. As I write this letter, numerous individuals are responding to this suspension with attacks on feminism, Tufts feminists, and those of us that work to combat the pervasive culture of rape on college campuses. These retaliatory attacks against "batshit crazy feminists and delusional school administrators" are inappropriate and misplaced. They are also highly detrimental to everything for which we stand, far more detrimental than the jersey in question. Suspending members of the men's crew team for a jersey that neither threatens nor implies any non-consensual sexual actions, let alone rape, only lessens our ability to strongly punish those who do threaten and imply such actions.

I am a proud Tufts Alum, an adamant feminist, and an individual with (colloquially) a cock. I request that you rescind the decision to suspend these individuals and defer the complaints you have received to the TCUJ or other student-led arbitration body for due process. Not doing so is a disservice to our school, to the fundamental rights of those involved, and to the true fight against the misogynistic rape culture that is currently profiting from these actions.

Sincerely Yours,
Andy Sayler
Tufts E11
